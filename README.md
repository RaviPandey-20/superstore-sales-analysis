# Superstore Sales Analysis

## Overview
Analyzed 9,994 retail transactions from a US-based superstore
to identify revenue drivers, loss-making products, and regional
growth opportunities.

## Tools Used
- Excel — data cleaning
- MySQL — data analysis
- Power BI — data visualization

## Business Questions Answered
1. Which regions drive the most revenue?
2. What are the top 10 most profitable products?
3. What are the monthly sales trends?
4. Which sub-categories are losing money?
5. Which customer segments are most valuable?

## Key Findings

### Regional Performance
- West region is the top performer driving 31% of total revenue ($713K)
- South region is the weakest at only 17% of total revenue ($388K)
- Total company revenue across all regions: $2,272,450

### Product Performance
- Canon imageCLASS 2200 Copier is the single most profitable 
  product generating $25,199 — 3x more than any other product
- Technology products (printers and copiers) dominate the 
  top 10 most profitable products

### Sales Trends
- Sales grew 92% over 4 years from $237K (2014) to $455K (2017)
- Q4 (Oct-Dec) consistently drives the highest sales every year
- January and February are the weakest months every year
- November 2017 was the single best month at $70,372

### Loss-Making Products
- 3 sub-categories are losing money totalling -$22,546 in losses
- Tables is the worst performer losing $17,725 despite high sales
- All 3 losing sub-categories are from Furniture and Office Supplies
- Recommend reviewing pricing strategy for Tables and Bookcases

### Customer Segments
- Consumer segment dominates with 52% of all orders (5,044 orders)
- Home Office customers spend the most per order at $246
- Corporate segment offers best overall value — high volume + high spend
